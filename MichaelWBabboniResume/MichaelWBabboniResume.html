Executive Summary

The Beginning…

Functional Development/Training Delivery (HCL)

I Begun my experience with HCL as a Billing consultant, by graduating from their internal ‘SAP - ISU/CR&B bootcamp. After that I was immediately  put to the test as a Billing consultant on a full implementation project at NFG (Buffalo, NY).  There - I was able to quickly hone the skills I would learn along the way. I spent 15 months on the project, starting at the beginning of the realization stage. I spent 11 months as a functional consultant, creating, articulating to various developers what was needed, tested, and passed onto prod environment. Those deliverables consisted of consisted of 6 Enhancements, 2 reports, 4 Interfaces 43 Forms - Configuring 3 rates - Presenting Demos on configuration, tutorials, design decisions, completed test runs within the Billing realm. I was awarded a company wide award from my work on this project as the ‘Unsung Hero’ - demonstrating a willingness to be a giver and help others in the background.  I was pivoted roles on the project to become the lead Billing & IDEX trainor. In this role I developed courses end-to-end, from the delivery, to the exercises, to the tests and all the administration and research work in between.

From NFG, I was sent to CMS (Jackson, MI). There I would become a full SAP knowledge based resource. I was given the role of a ‘Test Lead’, but found myself day-to-day doing mostly the training of other testers and planning the execution of the testing process. We were testing a 3rd party customer portal being implemented through a 3rd party vendor. I was basically the bridge of communication from the 3rd party consultants to the testers. I was very proud of how I was able to help facilitate a 3 month testing/high pressure situation and was able to successfully complete on time.

The Present…

Bill(Avertra)

I then joined Avertra, Jan 2016, where I was immediately put into a redesign project for the Budget Billing component at PNG (Pittsburgh, PA). I took a small role but vital, testing in this project. Internal SAP implementation. I configured the DM module of our internal SAP system.

After that, I was transitioned to the Product development side of the company. First, becoming a BA on the MiAgent implementation project, starting from the blueprint to the realization stage. My day-to-day for the beginning months were Rapid Prototyping - Designing the end-to-end interface of the  MiAgent application using a Rapid Prototyper tool (iRise).

 After I had finished building out the prototype for MiAgent (a replacement CRM) I was then the lead UI/UX designer of ‘MiBOB’ the Back office billing product - inspired  to create a more friendly User interface and experience for the Back office Billing component of SAP including the handling of BPEM, Bill/Invoice outsorts, and implausible meter reads.

After I had completed that,  I then began to build the ‘Wiki-based’ training platform for the MiAgent application using Mediawiki framework, mostly through HTML/CSS coding. Parallel to this I was enrolled, and completed a 2 month academy for to become a Freelance web developer. This academy was composed of a syllabus that included HTML, CSS, git, command line, JavaScript, and Jquery.
